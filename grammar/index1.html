Present Simple — форма та вживання

Ствердження, заперечення, запитання, signal words

Present Simple
POSITIVE
SENTENCE 		I / You / We / They + V
He / She / It + Vs

I work in Green Forest.
She reads books every day.
NEGATIVE
SENTENCE 		I / You / We / They + don’t + V
He / She / It + doesn’t +V

We don’t work on Sundays.
She doesn’t like coffee.
QUESTION Do + I / you / we / they + V?
Does + he / she / it + V?

Where do you work?
Does he study with us?
USE
Repeated actions or daily routines
e.g.: He usually starts work at 9 a.m.

General truths or laws of nature
e.g.: The Earth goes around the sun.

Signal words
Never, Not often / Rarely, Sometimes / Occasionally, Often, Usually, Always
Present Continuous
POSITIVE
SENTENCE 		I + am (‘m) + Ving
He / She / It + is (‘s) + Ving
You / We / They + are (‘re) Ving

I'm listening to music.
She is dancing.
We are playing the piano.
NEGATIVE
SENTENCE 		I + am not (‘m not) + Ving
He / She / It + is not (isn’t) + Ving
You / We / They + are not (aren’t) + Ving

I'm not watching the news.
He's not doing his homework.
You are not eating a tuna sandwich.
QUESTION Am+ I + Ving
Is + he / she / it + Ving
Are + you / we / they + Ving

Am I listening to music?
Is he watching the movie?
Are we going home?
USE
For actions happening right now at the moment of speaking
e.g.: He is reading a book now.
We are listening to the teacher now.

For the temporary actions happening around now, but not at the actual moment of speaking
e.g.: He isn’t going to the gym these days, because he is ill.

Signal words
Listen! Look! At the moment, now, these days, this term / year
-
Use it right!

Avoid double-negative sentences in English, for example sentences with not and never together:

Example:

  • I don’t never lie. ✗
  • I don’t lie. ✓
  • I never lie. ✓
